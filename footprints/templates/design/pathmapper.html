PATHMAPPER

Pathmapper is a web-based application for charting the historical journey of Jewish books across the world, and through time.

Using the Footprints database, Pathmapper traces the movements of these literary works on a map. These paths reveal compelling narratives and dissemination patterns of knowledge, faith, and culture in the global Diaspora.

Mapping Footprints

Mapping Footprints

Users will be able to select a footprint location and see all of the books known to have resided in that location, including where they came from, and in some cases where they went. In this example, users can see the paths of books in the Oppenheim collection presently at the Bodleian Library in Oxford, UK.

The timespan parameter allows users to focus the visualization on a certain period. The size of points on the map represents the number of books at the location within the specified time. The visualization can be further refined using other parameters in the filtering tool, such as imprints and ownership. Users can select one path on the map to view the movement and historical details of a any book across its footprint locations.

Comparing Timelines

Comparing Timelines

The Pathmapper Timeline offers users a way to analyze the paths of two or more books in the Footprints database. Users can select multiple paths of books on the map and see their representation over a particular time and place in the Timeline window.
